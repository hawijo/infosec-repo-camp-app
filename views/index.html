How telematics saves lives

Industry-leading hardware


The first step is to install a Cartrack telematics unit in your vehicle. Our advanced devices, developed in-house, are equipped with sophisticated sensors that enable data about your vehicle’s speed, location, acceleration and G-force impact to be reliably transmitted to our servers. This data is then securely analysed in real-time for any high-collision events or suspicious behaviour.

Immediate response


If an accident occurs, our highly trained support team will promptly contact yourself or any listed emergency contact of your choice. When you indicate that an accident has occurred, we will call emergency services and give them your exact location for the fastest response possible. In the event that you or your emergency contacts cannot be reached, your prior approved emergency services will be dispatched to your location, making sure that you always get the help you need.

Advanced AI analysis


Instant analysis takes place on-board the device when a high-collision event has taken place. Our unit automatically sends your vehicle’s data, including its GPS position, to our 24/7 control room where purposely designed algorithms detect whether a serious accident has occurred, if emergency services are needed or whether it is simply a fender-bender in a car park. This analysis takes place in a matter of seconds, and allows for more precise positioning of the accident.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Vehicle Accident Emergency Alert System?

It's a system designed to automatically detect car accidents, pinpoint the location, and send immediate alerts to emergency services and pre-determined contacts.

How will this system reduce accidents?

While the primary focus is on post-accident response, the system can also contribute to accident prevention by encouraging safer driving habits.

How will this system impact emergency services?

It will significantly improve response times and efficiency for emergency services.

Can the system detect different types of accidents (e.g., rollover, rear-end collision)?

Yes, the system is engineered to detect a wide range of accident types, including rollovers, rear-end collisions, and side impacts.

What happens if the system sends a false alert?

The system is designed to minimize false alerts by giving an alert to the driver and waiting for them to cancel the report if it is a false alarm.

Is my personal data protected?

We prioritize data privacy. Your personal information is securely stored and protected in accordance with data protection regulations.
